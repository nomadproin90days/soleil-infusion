What It Does & Why It Exists

When a consumer submits the website contact form or receives the b2c-lead tag, this workflow delivers an immediate welcome SMS and branded email, then nurtures them over 11 days with strategic touch points. At day 1, it checks for a booking — routing converted leads into the pre-visit workflow (WF-04) and sending non-bookers a direct booking link. At day 4, an educational email is sent. By day 11, persistent non-bookers are moved to a nurture pipeline stage.

Korean-language parallel branch is architected for the lang-korean tag — when Korean-language messaging is configured, the workflow delivers Korean-language versions of the welcome SMS and email automatically.

Business Problem It Solves

Web leads are the coldest type of inquiry. Without a structured nurture sequence, these leads convert at very low rates. The Korean-American community aged 35-65 is Soleil Infusion's core market — they respond well to consistent, respectful follow-up communication that respects their timeline and doesn't feel pushy.

Automation Steps
Step 1 — Welcome SMS
Immediate. "Hi [Name], thanks for your interest in Soleil Infusion! A team member will reach out shortly. Reply STOP to unsubscribe." (Korean version if lang-korean tag present.)

Immediate. Branded email with services overview, pricing summary, booking link, and location details.
Step 3 — Pipeline Opportunity
B2C Lead Pipeline, Stage: New Lead. Immediate tracking visibility.
Day 1 — Booking Check
Has contact booked? YES → Enroll in WF-04. NO → Booking link SMS: "Ready to experience Soleil Infusion? Book here: [link]"
Day 4 — Educational Email
"What to Expect at Soleil Infusion" — wellness lifestyle focus, no PHI, no medical claims. Builds trust and addresses the "What is IV therapy?" objection.
Day 11 — Long-term Nurture
If still no booking: Move opportunity to "Nurture" stage. Assign manual outreach task to team.
